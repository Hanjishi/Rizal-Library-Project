Lesson 1: The Alonso Realonda-Mercado Rizal Family

Introduction

The Rizal family played a crucial role in shaping José Rizal's character, values, and intellectual development. This lesson explores the family background, their influence on Rizal's life, and their contributions to Philippine society.

Family Background

The Rizal family was a prominent family in Calamba, Laguna, known for their education, wealth, and social standing. The family name "Rizal" was adopted from the Spanish word "ricial," meaning "green field."

Family Members:

  • Father: Francisco Mercado Rizal (1818-1898)
  • Mother: Teodora Alonso Realonda (1827-1911)
  • Siblings: 11 children in total, with José being the seventh

Parents' Influence

Both of Rizal's parents had significant influence on his development:

  • Francisco Mercado Rizal:
    • Educated in Latin and Philosophy
    • Managed the family's agricultural business
    • Instilled values of hard work and responsibility
  • Teodora Alonso Realonda:
    • Highly educated and cultured
    • First teacher of José Rizal
    • Influenced his love for literature and arts

Siblings and Family Life

The Rizal family was known for their close-knit relationships and intellectual pursuits:

  • Saturnina (Neneng) - Eldest sister, helped in Rizal's education
  • Paciano - Older brother, influenced Rizal's political views
  • Narcisa (Sisa) - Helped preserve Rizal's works
  • Olympia (Ypia) - Close to Rizal during his childhood
  • Lucia - Married to Mariano Herbosa
  • Maria (Biang) - Died at a young age
  • Concepcion (Concha) - Died at the age of 3
  • Josefa (Panggoy) - Never married
  • Trinidad (Trining) - Preserved Rizal's last poem
  • Soledad (Choleng) - Youngest sister

Family's Impact on Rizal's Development

The Rizal family's influence on José Rizal's life was profound:

  • Early education and intellectual development
  • Formation of moral values and character
  • Exposure to literature and the arts
  • Understanding of social issues and reform
  • Development of nationalist sentiments
